# ISO/IEC 27001 Internal Information Security Audit Report

**Organization:** Mr. Fix Phone Repair Shop  
**Industry:** Small Technology & Device Repair  
**Audit Title:** ISO/IEC 27001 Internal Information Security Audit  
**Audit Date:** 12 August 2025  
**Auditor:** Rustam Gadirov  

---

## 1. Audit Scope
This internal audit evaluated the organization’s alignment with selected ISO/IEC 27001 Annex A controls. The scope included:

- Governance and information security policies  
- Operational and technical security  
- Physical and environmental security  
- Access control practices  
- Communications and data protection  
- Supplier management  
- Incident management  
- Business continuity  
- Legal & regulatory compliance  

The audit focused on current operations, customer data handling, device repair workflows, and administrative processes.

---

## 2. Audit Objective
The audit aimed to determine whether implemented information security controls:

- Are appropriately designed  
- Are effectively implemented  
- Reduce risk to customer data and business operations  
- Support compliance with ISO/IEC 27001 requirements  

---

## 3. Audit Methodology
The audit was conducted using:

- Staff & technician interviews  
- Facility inspection (front desk, back office, storage, repair area)  
- Observation of operational workflows  
- Review of available documentation  
- Assessment of technical configurations where applicable  

---

## 4. Overall Assessment
Mr. Fix Phone Repair Shop demonstrates basic awareness of information security needs; however, several ISO 27001 control gaps were identified across multiple domains.

Major weaknesses include:

- Absence of formal policies & procedures  
- Weak access control and password management  
- No incident reporting or tracking  
- No documented business continuity or disaster recovery plan  
- No vendor risk management  
- Customer data stored without controls  
- Unsecured communications channels  

These gaps elevate the risk of:

- Data exposure  
- Service disruption  
- Financial loss  
- Legal noncompliance  
- Reputational damage  

Immediate remediation is recommended.

# 6. Detailed Findings

## Finding 1 — Lack of Documented Incident Reporting Process  
**ISO Control:** A.16 – Information Security Incident Management  
**Risk Level:** High  

**Issue:**  
Employees verbally report incidents; no formal process exists.

**Evidence:**  
No incident reports, logs, forms, or procedures available.

**Impact:**  
Incidents may go unreported, unmanaged, or repeated.

**Recommendation:**  
Establish a formal incident response procedure and maintain an incident log. Train employees on reporting.

---

## Finding 2 — No Business Continuity or Disaster Recovery Plan  
**ISO Control:** A.17 – Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery  
**Risk Level:** High  

**Issue:**  
No documented continuity or recovery procedures.

**Evidence:**  
No backup procedures, RTO/RPO objectives, or DR plans.

**Impact:**  
High risk of prolonged outages, data loss, and financial impact.

**Recommendation:**  
Develop a complete BCP/DRP and test it annually.

---

## Finding 3 — Customer Data Stored Without Privacy Controls  
**ISO Control:** A.18 – Compliance  
**Risk Level:** High  

**Issue:**  
Customer personal data is written in a physical notebook without consent, security, or retention rules.

**Evidence:**  
Notebook with customer data visible to staff and visitors.

**Impact:**  
Legal noncompliance, privacy violations, reputational damage.

**Recommendation:**  
Replace paper logs with secure digital systems and implement a privacy policy compliant with applicable laws.

# 8. Management Recommendations

1. Develop and document core ISO 27001 policies  
2. Establish access control standards  
3. Implement incident management & maintain logs  
4. Create a business continuity & DR plan  
5. Adopt encrypted communication channels  
6. Implement vendor risk assessments  
7. Train staff on awareness & privacy  
8. Perform quarterly internal audits

This audit report provides a clear roadmap to improve the organization’s information security posture.
