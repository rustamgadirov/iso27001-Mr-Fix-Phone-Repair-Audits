# Mr Fix Phone Repair – Incident Report  
### ISO 27001 A.5 / A.6 / A.7 / A.8

**Incident Title:** Employee Damage to Customer iPhone  
**Date of Incident:** 08/15/2025  
**Reported By:** Josh K. (Hardware Technician)  
**Time Reported:** 1:25 PM  

---

## **Incident Description**  
During a back glass repair procedure using the laser machine, a new employee failed to cover the customer’s iPhone rear camera with the protective tool. As a result, the laser came into contact with the camera module, causing permanent damage.

---

## **Evidence**  
- Damaged camera module observed during post-repair inspection  
- Technician statement provided  
- Laser machine operating log confirms repair session  
- No protective cover was documented on workstation checklist  

---

## **Impact**  
The iPhone’s rear camera became non-functional, resulting in additional motherboard damage and loss of customer data. This incident created financial loss, reputational damage, and noncompliance with ISO 27001 training and operational control requirements.

---

## **Root Cause**  
- New employee did not follow required back glass repair procedures.  
- Lack of formal training and supervision from management.  

---

## **Corrective Actions**  
1. Require all new employees to be supervised by an experienced technician during all repairs.  
2. Mandatory weekly review of repair procedures for all trainees until competency is verified.  

---

## **Preventive Actions**  
1. Post printed iPhone back glass repair procedures next to the laser machine for immediate reference.  
2. Evaluate and upgrade laser machine software to include automated safety prompts for sensitive components.
